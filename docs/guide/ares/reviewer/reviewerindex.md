## How to log in

Log in to the **ARES RE System** by entering your username and password. Typically, you will need to reset your password upon **first use**. Click the <Badge type="tip" text="Forgot Your Password" /> to reset.

:::: steps
1. **Step 1**: Obtain a reset link via email from <span style="color:#87CEFA">review@aressystem.org</span> by clicking Reset Password.

::: demo-wrapper img no-padding
   ![A11](/guide/ares/reviewer/fig/A11.jpg)
:::

2. **Step 2**: Enter new passwords and log in again to the main page of the ARES RE System.

::: demo-wrapper img no-padding
   ![A12](/guide/ares/reviewer/fig/R12.jpg)

::::

## Not receiving emails

If you cannot receive emails from the ARES system, you may try the following methods:

1. Check the junk mail folder by searching: <span style="color:#87CEFA">review@aressystem.org</span>.
2. Switch browsers. Recommend Google or Firefox browser.
3. When using the browser, recommend selecting incognito mode.

::: demo-wrapper img no-padding
   ![R19](/guide/ares/reviewer/fig/R19.jpg)
:::

4. If your email security settings are too high, the above suggestions may not work. Please contact the Managing Editor at the journal’s email address.

## No manuscript(s) available for review

1. Notification of non-review from the ARES RE System

When you cannot find the manuscript(s) you are assigned to review, it may have been removed from your review list. Once removed, you will receive a system notification.

2. Reason for removal

- he Managing Editor (ME) does not receive a response for a long time.
- Identify a conflict of interest with the author(s).
- Automatic closure of the review channel upon sufficient comments.

::: note
If the review channel closes automatically after sufficient comments have been collected, you are welcome to submit your feedback via email. If needed, the Managing Editor will forward your comments to the authors.
:::
